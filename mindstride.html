The problem

01 / Problem

Delayed Access

Most EAP appointments are booked weeks or months out. By the time an employee gets seen, a manageable problem has become a crisis — costing your business in absenteeism and turnover.

02 / Problem

Generalist Approach

Traditional EAPs assign whoever is available, not whoever is right. Employees end up with generic guidance that doesn't account for their culture, language, or specific situation — so they disengage.

03 / Problem

Reactive & Outdated

Legacy EAPs were designed for crisis response only. They don't invest in prevention, ongoing support, or measurement — so employers can't tell if they're making any difference at all.
